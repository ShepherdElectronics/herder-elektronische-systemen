Phase sensing, regeneration and long-term testability

Phase sensing, regeneration and
long-term testability.

The phase-sense network was documented to preserve the dynamometer’s operating knowledge and make the stations easier to maintain, troubleshoot and continue through future laboratory use.

Each BOOSTXL-DRV8305EVM motor phase is sensed individually relative to ground. The network uses a 62.0 kΩ upper resistor and a 4.99 kΩ lower resistor, giving a divider ratio of approximately 0.07449 V/V. With a 24 V DC bus, the expected range at each VSEN_A, VSEN_B or VSEN_C input is therefore approximately 0 to 1.79 V.

A 0.1 µF capacitor at each VSEN node forms a low-pass filter with the divider’s approximate 4.62 kΩ Thevenin resistance. The resulting time constant is approximately 462 µs, with a cutoff frequency near 344 Hz. Each VSEN signal should therefore appear as a filtered representation of the phase-to-ground switching behavior rather than as a sharp PWM waveform.

The board also uses D2, an onsemi NUP4201MR6T1G low-capacitance TVS/ESD protection array, on the low-voltage sensing side. Its purpose is to protect the VSEN and control electronics from electrostatic discharge and short transient overvoltage events. It does not absorb sustained regenerated energy, and it is not a brake resistor, DC-bus clamp or energy-dissipation element.

That distinction is essential in a dual-motor dynamometer. During regenerative operation, the load motor acts as a generator and returns electrical energy through the inverter to the shared DC bus. If the power supply cannot absorb that returned energy, the bus voltage can rise even while the individual VSEN inputs remain protected from short transients.

Regenerated energy must therefore be managed separately. Depending on the final station architecture, that may require a regenerative supply, a braking resistor with a controlled dump circuit, a dedicated DC-bus clamp, temporary energy storage or operating limits that keep the bus within its safe range.

The phase-sense network supports measurement and diagnosis. The TVS array protects the low-voltage sensing electronics. Neither one replaces the hardware required to manage power returned to the DC bus.

Documenting these separate functions gives future students and maintainers a basis for distinguishing among sensing faults, ADC-range problems, transient events and genuine DC-bus overvoltage caused by regeneration.

The broader purpose is long-term laboratory continuity: preserving the assumptions, expected measurements and protection boundaries needed to keep the dynamometer stations understandable, repairable and repeatable rather than allowing that knowledge to remain only in source code or institutional memory.
