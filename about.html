About Herder Elektronische Systemen

Engineering systems that can be understood, tested and trusted.

Herder Elektronische Systemen is an independent engineering practice focused on embedded controls, power electronics, real-time testing, technical recovery and AI-assisted engineering software.

01 / Origins

Built from a personal
power-electronics laboratory.

Herder Elektronische Systemen grew out of a personal engineering laboratory built by Jonathan Camfferman-Hernandez for hands-on work in power electronics, embedded control, electric machines, instrumentation and hardware validation.

What began as a collection of development boards, test equipment, power stages, motors and experimental circuits gradually became a complete working environment for developing and investigating real engineering systems.

The laboratory made it possible to work across the boundaries that often separate mechanical design, electronics, firmware, controls and measurement. A motor-drive problem could be followed from the power stage and current sensing through embedded firmware, communications, control models and measured test results.

As the projects became larger, another problem became equally important: preserving the files, evidence, decisions and documentation needed to understand and continue the work. That need led naturally into repository recovery, technical documentation and the development of AI-assisted engineering tools.

Herder Elektronische Systemen was formed from that combined practice: build the system, test the physical result, preserve the engineering evidence and leave the work in a state another engineer can understand.

The laboratory foundation

  • Embedded control hardware
  • Electric-machine test systems
  • PCB development and bring-up

03 / Engineering principles

Evidence keeps the
system honest.

01Evidence before claims

Describe what the hardware, test or record supports.

02Preserve system context

Keep interfaces, assumptions and constraints attached.

03Make changes traceable

Connect implementation changes to the decision they serve.

04Test the real integration

Exercise the path between code, hardware and measurement.

05Document uncertainty

State what remains unknown and what would resolve it.

04 / How the practice works

From boundary
to handoff.

01Define the engineering boundary

State what the system must do and what is in scope.

02Inspect the existing system and evidence

Review hardware, code, records and dependencies.

03Reproduce current behavior

Confirm what works, fails or remains unclear.

04Implement focused changes

Change the part that answers the next question.

05Validate and document the result

Leave measurements, limits and next actions visible.

06 / Founder and engineering lead

Jonathan Camfferman-Hernandez

Jonathan Camfferman-Hernandez is a mechanical engineer and the founder of Herder Elektronische Systemen. His work developed through years of independently building laboratory capability around power electronics, embedded systems, electric machines, precision motion, test automation and engineering software.

Rather than treating hardware, firmware and analysis as separate disciplines, he works through the complete engineering path: understanding the physical system, developing or recovering its electronics and code, instrumenting its behavior, evaluating the resulting data and documenting what the evidence supports.

His project work includes permanent-magnet motor-drive systems, field-oriented control, CAN-connected embedded platforms, power-converter prototypes, PCB design and bring-up, real-time test systems, precision motion equipment, automated instrumentation and recovery of complex technical repositories.

The same laboratory work also exposed a recurring problem: engineering projects often become difficult to continue because their files, decisions, dependencies and evidence are fragmented or poorly explained. That experience led to the development of SafeFileAI and SafeECADAI, tools intended to help inspect, recover and validate engineering project data while keeping automated conclusions connected to traceable evidence.

Jonathan established Herder Elektronische Systemen to bring this practical, cross-disciplinary approach to engineering projects that need more than isolated code, isolated hardware or surface-level documentation.

Working perspective

Build enough of the system to understand the interfaces.

Measure the physical behavior before making strong claims.

Keep software conclusions connected to engineering evidence.

Preserve uncertainty instead of concealing it.

Leave projects easier to inspect and continue than they were found.
