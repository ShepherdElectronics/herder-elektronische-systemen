HOME

Electronic systems, engineered at the level the problem requires.

Herder Elektronische Systemen develops, integrates, recovers and validates electronic systems across embedded firmware, motor control, power electronics, instrumentation and test.

The work may be as focused as selecting a cost-effective power supply, configuring an Arduino-class controller, correcting a communications interface or establishing a repeatable test. More complex engagements may involve custom flyback magnetics, mixed-signal PCB development, deterministic TI C2000 motor control, dual-core STM32 architecture, CAN-connected real-time testing or recovery of an incomplete hardware-software platform.

The practice supports R&D teams, laboratories, technical founders, manufacturers and specialized product developers that need hands-on engineering from initial bench work through documented validation.

THE CLASS OF PROBLEMS WE SOLVE

Difficult systems rarely fail inside one discipline.

A control problem may originate in sensing, timing, power delivery or physical response. Firmware may appear correct until it is exercised against the real hardware. A test result may be shaped as much by instrumentation and procedure as by the design itself.

Herder Elektronische Systemen works where those boundaries meet.

Typical engagements involve:

* embedded control that behaves differently on real hardware than in isolation
* incomplete or inherited systems that cannot be reproduced reliably
* motor-control, communications or instrumentation integration
* power-conversion prototypes requiring measured revision
* test procedures that produce data but not trustworthy conclusions
* technical projects whose files, hardware and documentation no longer agree
* early-stage products that need broad hands-on ownership without several separate specialists

The objective is to make the system understandable, testable and technically actionable.

THE RIGHT LEVEL OF ENGINEERING

Use the simplest solution that satisfies the real requirement.

Not every problem requires custom electronics.

A commercial power supply, standard module or development board may be the correct answer when its electrical, environmental and integration limits are understood. In other cases, the system may require custom firmware, a purpose-built PCB, deterministic control, custom magnetics or complete real-time validation.

Herder Elektronische Systemen works across that range.

WHY HERDER ELEKTRONISCHE SYSTEMEN

Cross-layer engineering without unnecessary complexity.

The practice does not force every problem into a custom architecture, and it does not stop at a development board when the application requires deeper engineering.

The differentiator is the ability to move between:

* schematic and PCB
* firmware and physical behavior
* controls and instrumentation
* power electronics and measurement
* source code and test evidence
* prototype operation and technical handoff

The goal is not merely to deliver code, a circuit or a report.

The goal is to leave the system more understandable, testable and transferable than it was at the start.

HOW ENGAGEMENTS WORK

A professional engagement begins with the technical boundary.

A premium client should be able to understand how the relationship starts, what information is required and what the first stage produces.

1. Initial inquiry

The first message should identify:

* the system or product
* its present stage
* the technical problem or decision
* available hardware
* available source files
* existing measurements
* expected deliverables
* schedule
* approximate budget range
* preferred working arrangement

Sensitive files are not required for the initial inquiry.

2. First technical discussion

The first discussion is used to determine:

* whether the problem fits the practice
* whether the available evidence is sufficient
* what additional access is needed
* which uncertainties should be resolved first
* whether the work should begin as an assessment, prototype task, recovery phase or larger development engagement

The first discussion is not a free design review.

Its purpose is to establish the technical and commercial boundary.

3. What the first discussion produces

When there is a credible fit, the discussion produces a written next step that may include:

* a concise problem definition
* the proposed scope
* required inputs
* expected deliverables
* responsibilities
* assumptions
* exclusions
* schedule
* estimated effort or project price
* acceptance conditions
* confidentiality requirements

Where the technical state is too uncertain for a complete project scope, the first paid phase may be a technical assessment.

4. NDA and confidential information

Mutual or client-provided NDAs can be reviewed before sensitive technical material is exchanged.

Confidential project files should be transferred only through an agreed secure method.

Herder Elektronische Systemen does not publish client work, client names, technical details or engagement outcomes without explicit authorization.

Public portfolio material consists of authorized work, personal engineering projects and clearly identified academic or research-facility work.

5. Geography and working arrangement

Herder Elektronische Systemen is based in Michigan, United States.

Most software, firmware, design review, analysis, documentation and technical-recovery work can be completed remotely.

Remote engagements may use:

* secure file exchange
* source-control access
* test data
* photographs and video
* instrument exports
* shipped hardware
* documented remote test procedures

On-site work may be considered when physical access is necessary for:

* prototype bring-up
* instrumentation
* embedded-system integration
* power-electronics testing
* motor-control systems
* laboratory recovery
* acceptance testing

Local and regional on-site work is most practical within Michigan and the greater Great Lakes region. Travel outside that area requires a defined scope, schedule and expense arrangement.

6. Response expectations

Initial inquiries are reviewed for technical fit, available evidence and scheduling feasibility.

Clients should expect:

* acknowledgement of a serious inquiry
* direct questions where important information is missing
* a clear indication of fit or non-fit
* written scope before substantive engineering begins
* documented findings rather than informal conclusions
* explicit identification of assumptions and uncertainty
* communication when scope, schedule or technical risk changes

Urgent or emergency work must be identified clearly and is accepted only when capacity and access permit.

PROJECTS NOT ACCEPTED

Clear exclusions protect both parties.

The practice does not accept:

* work requiring representation as a licensed professional engineer where licensure is legally required
* certification claims without an appropriate certification body
* final regulatory approval
* production qualification presented as complete without the required testing
* undocumented safety-critical deployment
* medical, aviation, automotive or industrial-safety claims beyond the agreed engineering evidence
* copying or reverse engineering proprietary systems without authorization
* work involving stolen, unlawfully obtained or restricted technical material
* requests to fabricate measurements, test results or compliance evidence
* projects where the client will not provide sufficient access to reproduce the issue
* engagements whose schedule or budget is incompatible with the required technical work
* open-ended emergency support without a defined scope
* production manufacturing commitments unless explicitly agreed
* guaranteed outcomes where the available hardware, evidence or access cannot support such a guarantee

Standards-inspired methods may be used where appropriate, but they are not presented as certification unless separately established.

ENGINEERING APPROACH

Engineering decisions made before they become expensive.

* requirements

* available evidence
* risks
* acceptance conditions

Architect

Define:

* system boundaries
* hardware and software ownership
* interfaces

* control strategy

* fault behavior

Build

* firmware
* electronics
* controls
* automation
* prototype integration
* targeted revisions

Validate

Measure:

* integrated behavior
* repeatability
* timing
* scaling
* physical response
* test limits
* remaining uncertainty

Hand off

Leave:

* source and build context
* interface documentation
* procedures
* evidence
* limitations
* prioritized next work

ABOUT

Engineering systems that can be understood, tested and continued.

Herder Elektronische Systemen is a U.S.-based independent engineering practice in Michigan.

The practice works across embedded firmware, electronics, controls, power conversion, instrumentation and technical recovery because the behavior of a prototype rarely belongs to only one layer.

The central method is straightforward:

* define the real technical boundary
* reproduce the relevant behavior
* measure the system
* connect conclusions to evidence
* preserve what is known
* state what remains uncertain
* leave a usable technical path forward
